The Peruvian Geotechnical Biennial is a national initiative led by the Peruvian National Group of the International Association for Engineering Geology and the Environment (IAEG Peru). It is held every two years and aims to promote geotechnical knowledge, collaboration, and innovation to support sustainable development and disaster risk reduction in Peru and across Latin America. The Biennial is open to students, professionals, academics, and institutions interested in geotechnical practice and education. It serves as a dynamic space for sharing cutting-edge developments, fostering collaboration between sectors, and building technical capacity in geotechnics, engineering geology, and related fields. 🎯 Purpose and Vision The Biennial seeks to: Promote best practices in geotechnical and engineering geology. Provide a platform for dialogue between academia, government, and industry. Showcase national and regional advances in risk reduction and sustainable development. Support inclusive participation, especially of young professionals and underrepresented groups. 🔹 2024 Edition Recap – 1st Peruvian Geotechnical Biennial “Geotechnics for Prevention and Sustainability” Held at the National University of Engineering (UNI) in Lima, the inaugural edition marked a milestone for IAEG Peru and featured national and regional speakers, training sessions, and the Latin American Symposium on Geoethics. → [Link to summary] (Coming soon) 🔹 2026 Edition – Coming Soon Plans for the 2nd Biennial are currently underway.
